Great Wall of China

The structures portions of the wall were built during the rule of the Subhio + + rulers, the new construction was feared to have been waged by nomadic tribes of the northern peoples. After the unification of China by Qin Shi Huang in the year (221 BC), the construction of the wall accelerated, and the works ended in (204 BC), after more than 300,000 people participated in it, the Han dynasty (202 BC). ), then Sui (589 - 618 AD) construction work. The Ming Dynasty (1368 - 1644 AD) contributed to extending and strengthening the wall, and the parts built with mud were replaced by brick buildings. The construction reached its final length (6,700 km) and ran parallel to neighboring rivers and its bends were shaped by the terrain of the mountains and hills it traverses. The Great Wall of China was added to its World Heritage List in 1980.
